Design Improvements
1
Real name & avatar placeholder — Use initials + coloured avatar instead of a broken icon; add a verified badge to signal trust.
2
Social proof above the fold — Star rating + review count immediately communicates quality before the user scrolls.
3
Scannable stat strip — Experience / Students / Satisfaction in a 3-column grid replaces the vague "12 J." label with meaningful context.
4
Availability pulse — A live dot next to "Available now" creates urgency and removes friction to booking.
5
Primary CTA hierarchy — "Book Lesson" is the dominant filled button; "Message" is secondary. The original design had equal-weight actions competing for attention.
6
Colour-coded tag taxonomy — Blue = skills, purple = languages, green = badges. At a glance the user knows what each tag means without reading every word.
7
Destructive action de-emphasised — "Remove instructor" is a ghost text-link at the bottom, preventing accidental taps while remaining discoverable.
