## What I understand

The core issue is **two architectural problems bundled together:**

**1. Security / layering:** The UI (`student.js`, `teacher.js`, `ui.js`) directly calls `ProfileStore.getPrice()` — that is a direct store read that bypasses `AppService`. When this migrates to Firestore, that would be a direct DB call from the browser UI. Everything must go through `AppService`.

**2. Missing service functions:** The deposit calculation logic exists (`_calcDeposit` in adapter) but is completely private. The UI has no way to ask "given these pending bookings for this teacher, what deposit is required, and can the student afford it?" — that entire question has no answer in the service layer.
